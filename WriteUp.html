Love Letter AI

A web application that allows users to play full games of Love Letter against a computer AI.

1. Introduction

About a year and a half ago I was introduced to many different card games. One that I instantly fell in love with was Love Letter. Love letter is a game that involves chance, memory, and risk. To play this game each player is dealt one card and then another on their turn. There are 16 cards total and each card has a special way it can be used and a point value. The whole objective is to either eliminate people using the special instructions of each card or be that last one left with the highest point valued card. The fact that each turn and all the different possibilities of each card I thought it would be very interesting to make a two player game using web programming languages.

5 The Game

To play the game there are a lot of rules and very specific rules so here is a link: Love Letter Rules. In this write up however I will briefly explain the concept of a two player game. First you start off with a deck which consist of 16 cards and of those there are 8 different possible cards:
Card Name Point Value # of cards per deck Description
Guard 1 5 Name a non-Guard card and choose a player, if that player has that card, he or she is out of the round.
Priest 2 2 Look at another player's hand.
Baron 3 2 You and another player secretly compare hands. The player with the lower value looses
Handmaid 4 2 Until your next turn, ignore all effects from other players' cards
Prince 5 2 Choose any player (including yourself) to discard his or her hand and draw a new card.
King 6 1 Trade Hands with another player
Countess 7 1 If you have this card and the King or Prince in your hand, you must discard this card
Princess 8 1 If you discard this card, you lose.

The way the game starts is that 4 cards are automatically taken out of the deck, 3 of those cards are displayed while the other one is hidden and you cannot see what it is. Then the deck deals a card to you and the computer. I have programmed it so that the user always starts first. So one every turn you are dealt another card. The user then must choose a card to play and depending on the card the game may keep going. It goes back and forth until a card is used to win or the deck is empty.

If the deck is empty the user and the computer then compare cards and the card with the higher point value wins a token of affection but if the cards are equal then it goes to a tie breaker. The tie breaker will add up the played cards value of each player and the player with the higher value wins a token of affection.

Once a player reaches 4 tokens of affection they win the whole game and then the game will be restarted.
